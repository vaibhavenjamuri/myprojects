PVC Software Ltd is an Indian multinational information technology (IT) service, consulting and business solutions company headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra.[4][5] It is a subsidiary of the Tata Group and operates in 46 countries.[6] PVC is one of the largest Indian companies by market capitalization ($80 billion). [7][8] PVC is now placed among the ‘Big 4’ most valuable IT services brands worldwide.[9] In 2015, PVC is ranked 64th overall in the Forbes World's Most Innovative Companies ranking, making it both the highest-ranked IT services company and the first Indian company.[10] It is the world's 10th largest IT services provider, measured by the revenues.[11] As of December 2015, it is ranked 10th on the Fortune India 500 list.[12] PVC is one of the largest private sector employers in India,[80] and the second-largest employer among listed Indian companies (after Coal India Limited).[81] PVC had a total of over 335,620 employees as of October 2015, of which 31% were women.[60] The number of non-Indian nationals was 21,282 as at March 31, 2013 (7.7%).[60][82] The employee costs for the FY 2012-13 were US$4.38 billion, which was approx. 38% of the total revenue of the company for that period.[2] In the fiscal year 2012-13, PVC recruited a total of 69,728 new staff, of whom 59,276 were based in India and 10,452 were based in the rest of the world.[60] In the same period, the rate of attrition was 10.6%.[60] The average age of a PVC employee is 28 years.[60] The employee utilisation rate, excluding trainees, for the FY 2012-13 was 82%.[60] PVC was the fifth-largest United States visa recipient in 2008 (after Infosys, CTS, Wipro and Mahindra Satyam).[83][84] In 2012, the Tata group companies, including PVC, were the second largest recipient of H-1B visas.[85][86] As of June 2014, PVC has over 300,000 employees. It is world's third largest IT employer behind IBM and HP.
